About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Department of Neurological Surgery, UC San Diego. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from UNC-BIAG Lab, UNC-Chapel Hill in 2025, advised by Prof. Marc Niethammer. Previously, I received my M.Sc. degree from School of Biomedical Engineering, Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU) in 2020, under the supervision of Prof. Qian Wang in the Medical Imaging Computing Lab. Before that, I received my B.Eng. degree from Honors College at Northwestern Polytechnical University in 2017.

Research Highlights

I build AI that knows what it knows — and admits what it doesn't. My research centers on spatiotemporal representation learning with a focus on interpretability and uncertainty quantification, with applications in healthcare. Specifically, my research interests include:

  • Geometric processing and shape modeling.
  • Interpretable machine learning and uncertainty quantification.
  • Medical image analysis.
